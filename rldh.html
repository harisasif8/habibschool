CPPL Conference – July 15th – 19th 2019

Day wise Testimony

Habib Public School is always a strong advocate of the training and professional development of their staff. The Professional development can be provided in many ways. It can be made available through external expertise in the form of courses and workshops, through collaboration between schools or teachers, across schools or within the schools.

It is always an urgent educational need that teachers should receive adequate educational and professional training to possess adequate knowledge and teaching skills and to be able to dedicate themselves to the teaching profession. This can be provided through coaching/mentoring, collaborative planning and teaching, and the sharing of good practices.

HPS uses a variety of schedules to provide this collaborative learning and work time for teachers. The beginning of the new academic year 2019- 20 commenced with new progressions in the Staff Professional learning and personal learning by organizing the first CPPL conference from July 15 th – 19 th , 2019 under the name of the newly revived department for Teachers’ Professional Development. The glimpse of each day portrayed is the flashback for everyone to enjoy it again with the same essence.



Day 1 – July 15th 2019

Productivity is never an accident. It is always the result of a commitment to excellence, intelligent planning, and focused effort.”

The first day of CPPL conference pursued the legacy of excellence, began as a huge educational symposium in the gymnasium of Habib Public School, hosted by our eloquent CPPL Team Member Ms. Aqsa Akhter from College section.

The day started at 8:00 am with Meet and Greet over a cup of tea at green area. The schedule of day 1was planned with 2 keynote sessions as day beginners. The formal opening keynote of the conference was at 8:30 am, by our respected Principal Mrs. Ainee Shehzad. The 2nd key note session was conducted by our respected guest Ms. Siham Gheewala. She gave her presentation on “Learning to grow”, followed by a Question /Answers session.

The conference served an advantageous platform and great deal of time for our teaching staff to showcase their learning expertise. These 1 hour sessions were cascaded by our in-house teachers who willingly volunteered to shared their expertise through the various topics with rest of our staff members. At 10:30am, the entire staff was engaged at 6 different sessions of their choice. There were 6 different rooms allocated at the College section for the Professional Development sessions. The workshops were evaluated at the end of the sessions by the participants through the evaluation forms.

All the teachers gathered back at Gymnasium at 12:15am for Team building activity which was full of fun and energy. 16 groups were formed altogether, in which each group is a mix of teachers from each section to serve the core purpose of the activity which was to restore the unity and love amongst all members in a group so that they should feel they are all one, working together for HPS, achieving its 3 in 3 vision. The 6 facilitators of day 1were awarded with the certificates. At the end of the day the lunch was served at the green area, arranged by the newly confirmed staff members & the school leadership team at 1:30pm.

Overall, the first day of CPPL Conference went well, achieved all of its learning goals.



Day 2 – July 16th 2019

On July 16th , the 2nd day of the conference began with the keynote session by the renowned keynote speaker Mr. Ashok Kumar. His humorous but hard hitting style has inspired thousands and helped them making massive changes to their thinking processes and taking greater levels of personal responsibility than ever before in their life. In his session he emphasized on the role of teachers in the success of students.

Later, our very experienced teachers played a role of facilitators.. Ms. Fatima Rais, Ms. Bushra Wajid, Ms. Saboohi Raza, Ms. Tahira Kashif, Ms. Midhat Mustafain, Ms. Umme Kulsoom and Ms. Sadia Abbas were the facilitators for day 2. After the sessions, the team building activity was arranged to refresh the teachers. Every teacher wholeheartedly participated in the activity. The day ended with a vote of thanks from CPPL TEAM followed by lunch at green area.



Day 3 – July 17th 2019

On the 3rd day, the conference started with the key note session by our guest speaker Ms. Sonal Dhanani. She came up with the relaxing, energizing and innovative learning and teaching strategies in all the levels of classes. The learning out comes from this session were: 1. To make students more comfortable and kind towards other fellows. 2. To think more critically in tranquil way.

The mentioned professional development sessions were planned for day 3. 1. School literacy 2. Teaching to facilitating Learning 3. Ten ways to untwist your thinking 4. Motivation classroom learning management and team work 5. Disadvantages of technology. 6. Good body language improves class management.

These sessions were followed by the rejoicing, uniting and rejoining team building activities as the last segment of the day that stole the show. The lunch feast is the last part of the day enjoyed thoroughly by all.



Day 4 – July 18th 2019

On Thursday, July 18, 2019, the conference was hosted by Ms. Rabab Naqvi from Junior section. The programme began with a key note session by a renowned public speaker and educationist Fahad Ahmed at 8.30 a.m. sharp. Fahad Ahmed spoke about the Good management and how Six Hats methodology is a good decision making technique and method for group discussions and individual thinking. Furthermore how this technique gets combined with the parallel thinking process, this technique helps groups think more effectively.

At 10.30 a.m. sharp following six professional development sessions were organized at the HPS College section in different rooms to promote learning for teachers which ended at 12a.m.

Session 1: Get their Hands Dirty – A project-based learning. Session 2: Importance of Interactive Bulletin Boards

Session 3: Importance of Nonverbal Communication in teaching

Session 4: Proper Usage of Scientific calculator" Session 6: "Conflict management within the teams & In the last, team-building activity “The great egg drop” was arranged in which groups built structures and tried to prevent an egg from breaking. A judge tossed all structures (with the eggs inside) from a high elevation at the end. The winners were the groups that successfully protect the egg. This amazing activity added the pleasure of associating with the other members of Habib family from different sections. The day ended with the lunch at green area.



Day 5 – July 19th 2019

The last day of CPPL Conference was full of excitement and bundle of action. There was everything to suit everyone’s taste. It turned out to be a day HPS will always remember. The key note session was organized in gymnasium which was carried out by Danish Sayanee. He motivated the teachers through his presentation which went quite well. The teachers, then, moved to attend CPPL Sessions, executed by our diligent teachers.

The real fun began when everyone gathered in the gymnasium again for Team Building Activity. It was Treasure Hunt. The teachers were given time of 30mins to find thirty clues placed at different sites in the school. Every team had a different color code and they were to report back about their status at sharp 12. The team who reported on time with maximum number of clues, was considered as the winner.

The session was ended with closing ceremony. The teachers who volunteer their time to share their knowledge and held successful development sessions were awarded with certificates. This memorable day ended with melodic voice of Mr Asif, Mr Moosir , Mr Waqar and Ms Tahira who entertained the audience with beautiful tunes.



Concluding notes:

The overall aura of our very first CPPL – conference was considered a big success at the end, beside the entire HPS participation, the sister schools were also invited to be a part of our journey to excellence. Teachers from APWA Rana liaquat Ali Khan School and Green Shield Public School have participated in our conference.

However the genial regrets received from HGS and Ghulaman-e-Abbas school due to their commitments at the beginning of the new academic year of their schools on the same dates. The guest speakers invited for keynote sessions were imparted their best to ensure the quality of the presentation that is equally beneficial for the participants in terms of learning new concepts and methodologies with in a great deal of time. The sessions evaluations for our in-house facilitators were also served a positive note for their efforts as few of them have came up to showcase their learning for the very first time. The platform served as a booster to their confidence in sharing their expertise with the fellow staff members.

It was also a great honour to us to have Ms. Zehra Gheewala & Mr. Asad Ali as out -sourced facilitators for our professional development session part. The team building activity segment was the most enthralled part of all 5 days. The different mixed participations from every section at school in groups every day developed a harmony of unity, coherence and the chance of meeting the fellow colleagues of one big school generated the approach of being the part of one HPS family working together in promoting the school’s 3 in 3 vision.

The hard work of the facilitators, who conducted the Professional Development sessions were rewarded in the form of vote of thanks by RLD team lead and the certificates of appreciation that were given to them on the same day after the team building activity at the gymnasium.

Last but the most significant part of the all the days was to have lunch together at the end of the tiring day which was arranged by the staff for the staff. A great initiative was taken to celebrate the confirmation by the newly inducted teachers to their seniors as a token of love. The official exposure of the newly revived professional development department at HPS brought into recognition by organizing their first programme under its name, ‘Research, Learning & Development HUB. The remarkable collaboration since the planning, organizing and executing the conference of the entire RLD – Team was evident throughout the conference.

We are looking forward for the same in the days to come. We do expect the new learning and the refreshers will be transferred to the classrooms for the benefit of both the students and teachers.
